About Me

I was born a Virginian in the suburbs of Washington DC and I go back home for the holidays every year. Outside of that, I can be more difficult to find. I grew up living overseas in Athens and New Delhi, son of a State Department employee. My formative years were spent taking those experiences for granted (I was only a teenager) and playing video games.

I attended university at Tulane in New Orleans where I learned about music, sin, Mardi Gras, and hurricanes. I graduated a semester late because of Katrina with a degree in philosophy and classical history and a lot of drinking stories.

I spent a year abroad teaching English as an exchange teacher in Japan before returning to the Washington DC metro area. I parlayed my liberal arts degree into a tech job as a project manager at a web agency and that was the beginning of my first career as a web consultant and technical project manager.
